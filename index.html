Other coworking spaces

Most coworking spaces typically consist of large open workspaces or a type of shared office where someone can either buy into a membership or pay a fee in order to have a space to work. Usually you have three options: open seating (just find a free space and start working), dedicated desk or private office. Obviously, pricing will vary depending on the plan you choose and all coworking spaces differ as far as membership options, pricing and space available. However, what is pretty universal is the terminology. Terms such as collaborative workspace, community, flexibility, network, etc. are spewed out excessively with some delivering on those promises more than others but ultimately most are pretty much exactly what you would expect, simply just a place to plop down and get some work done.

Coworking Spaces vs Traditional Office Space

A lot of people ask what the difference is between coworking spaces and traditional office rental agencies. On paper there's not a whole lot anymore. Nowadays, most traditional agencies are trying to tap into the coworking market and advertise they offer coworking space. However, just because you have a few desks in a room where multiple people can work doesn't necessarily qualify as coworking. For us it really boils down to one thing, what are you selling. Are you selling people on office space or are you selling them on a community. You can have a hundred people in one room but if no one is collaborating or offering themself as a resource to anyone else in the office then, in our opinion, it just becomes a large room of wasted resources. Coworking isn't for everyone. Some people would rather stay to themselves and that's perfectly fine. In our experience though most people thrive when they are a part of a strong community and a community's strength is only as strong as the people in it. Sometimes we forget the accomplishments that can be made when we stick together!
